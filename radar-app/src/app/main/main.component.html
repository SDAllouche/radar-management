Registration

Registration is a crucial process for vehicle owners. It involves registering the vehicle with the government authority responsible for motor vehicle administration. During registration, information such as the make, model, and VIN is recorded, along with the owner's details. This process ensures that vehicles can be identified and traced, assists law enforcement, and facilitates taxation and insurance procedures. By registering your car, you comply with legal requirements and contribute to road safety and organization.

Radar

Radar is a technology that uses radio waves to measure the speed of moving objects. In transportation, radar is commonly used to monitor vehicle speeds. It helps law enforcement enforce speed limits, promoting road safety. Radar systems have evolved to be accurate and reliable, even in heavy traffic. By utilizing radar, authorities can effectively regulate speeds and ensure safer roads.

Violation

Violation occurs when a vehicle exceeds the speed limit detected by a radar device. It is a risky behavior that endangers the driver and others on the road. Radar technology allows law enforcement to measure and record these violations. Depending on the severity, penalties for radar violations may include fines, license points, or driving courses. By enforcing radar violations, authorities discourage reckless driving and create a responsible driving culture, ultimately enhancing road safety.
